How to read the pegs

This is the part that trips everyone up: the small pegs are a tally, not a map. They tell you how many of your guesses are right — never which ones.

Your guess
One black. Red is in the code and in the right spot — position 1.
One white. Yellow is in the code, but you put it in position 3 and it belongs in position 4.
Two blanks. Purple and orange aren't in the code at all.

Notice what the feedback doesn't say: that the black peg is about red. The pegs always sort themselves — blacks first, then whites — so their order carries no information. You get "one right, one misplaced" and have to work out the rest by comparing it against your next guess.

That's the whole game. Each guess narrows 1,296 possible codes down a little further, until only one can still fit everything you've seen.
